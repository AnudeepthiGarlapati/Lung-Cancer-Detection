Lung Cancer Classes

Normal Cases

Normal lung tissue refers to the healthy, non-cancerous cells and structures that make up the lungs. In a healthy individual, the lungs perform vital functions such as oxygen exchange and carbon dioxide removal.

Benign Cases

Benign lung tumors are non-cancerous growths that develop in the lungs. These tumors typically grow slowly and do not pose an immediate threat to health.

Malignant Cases

Malignant lung cancer refers to cancerous tumors that develop in the lungs. These tumors can be further classified into two main types: non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) and small cell lung cancer (SCLC).
